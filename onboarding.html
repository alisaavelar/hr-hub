Onboarding

Every new hire deserves a great first week. This skill builds it for them — and you — before their start date.
1 Skill Cowork Plugin Stage 02 of 04

Why This Matters

The first 90 days determine whether a new hire becomes a contributor or a question mark. Most onboarding fails not because of bad intentions, but because nobody has time to build a thoughtful plan. This skill does it in one prompt. You describe the role and the person. It builds the pre-start checklist, Day 1 schedule, Week 1 agenda, and 30/60/90-day goals — all ready to share.

What Gets Built

Pre
Pre-Start Checklist
Everything that needs to happen before the new hire walks in — accounts provisioned, equipment ordered, buddy assigned, first-week invites sent, manager prep done. No more scrambling on Day 1.
D1
Day 1 Schedule
A structured hour-by-hour first day — welcome meeting, team intros, systems walkthrough, and a clear end-of-day check-in. First impressions become great ones.
W1
Week 1 Agenda
Key meetings, documentation to read, people to meet, and first small wins to pursue. Designed so new hires feel oriented, not overwhelmed.
90d
30 / 60 / 90-Day Goals
Role-specific milestone goals for the first three months. Clear enough to evaluate progress, realistic enough to achieve, ambitious enough to matter.

30 / 60 / 90-Day Framework

Day 30
Learn
Understand the org, the team, the tools, and the work. Ask questions. Ship nothing major. Build context.
Day 60
Contribute
Identify quick wins. Take ownership of a defined area. Share early findings with the team.
Day 90
Lead
Drive an initiative independently. Set your own priorities. Onboarding is complete.

Trigger Phrases

"Create onboarding plan for [name]" "[Name] starts Monday — build their first week" "30/60/90 goals for a new [role]" "Pre-start checklist for [name]" "What needs to happen before [name]'s start date?"
