People Operations

Headcount reports, policy answers, and org design — everything that keeps the people engine running smoothly.
3 Skills Cowork Plugin Stage 04 of 04

Why This Matters

Running people operations means answering a hundred questions a week — "what's our headcount?", "can someone work remotely from another state?", "should we hire another PM or a senior IC?" These skills turn those questions into instant, accurate answers. No more digging through policy docs or building yet another headcount spreadsheet from scratch.

Three Skills in This Stage

📊
People Report
Headcount snapshots, attrition by team, diversity representation, and org health metrics — ready for leadership in minutes.
📋
Policy Lookup
Plain-language answers to any policy question — PTO, remote work, expense limits, leave, benefits — without hunting through the handbook.
🏗️
Org Planning
Model headcount scenarios, design team structures, and think through spans of control and reporting relationships with a thought partner who knows what good looks like.

People Report: What Gets Built

👥
Headcount snapshot. Total headcount by team, department, and level — formatted for a leadership update or board deck.
📉
Attrition analysis. Turnover trends by team with voluntary vs. involuntary breakdowns and tenure analysis. Know where you're losing people and why.
🌍
Diversity metrics. Representation by race, gender, and seniority level — with the context needed to have honest conversations with leadership.

Trigger Phrases

SkillExample Phrases
People Report "What's our current headcount?" · "Attrition report for Q1" · "Diversity breakdown by department" · "Pull a headcount snapshot for the board deck"
Policy Lookup "What's our PTO policy?" · "Can [name] work remotely from Texas?" · "How does parental leave work?" · "What's the expense reimbursement limit for travel?"
Org Planning "Help me think through headcount for next quarter" · "Should I add a manager layer or keep the team flat?" · "What's a reasonable span of control for a VP?" · "Model two org structures for the Product team"

Org Planning: Common Scenarios

🔀
Reorgs. Think through the trade-offs of restructuring a team — who reports to whom, what changes, and how to communicate it.
🎯
Next hire decisions. Given the team's current shape and goals, should you hire a senior IC, a generalist, or a manager? This skill helps you reason through it.
📈
Scaling plans. Model what the team needs to look like in 6, 12, and 18 months to hit your goals — with phased headcount and role prioritization.
